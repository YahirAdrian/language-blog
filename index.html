References

Chiswick, B. R., & Miller, P. W. (2005). Linguistic Distance: A Quantitative Measure of the Distance Between English and Other Languages. Journal of Multilingual and Multicultural Development, 26(1), 1–11. https://doi.org/10.1080/14790710508668395 This article is about the linguistic distance that makes it difficult for English speakers to learn another language. It is said that it cannot be measured qualitatively or quantitatively, but it is developed a quantitative scale to analyze the difficulty of learning other languages. Language distance differs by country of origin, language influence, migration causes, family trees, prior knowledge (if previously taught in schools), geographic distance, purpose for learning the language, etc.

Woolston, C. & Osório, J. (2019). When English is not your mother tongue. Nature, 570(7760), 265-267. https://doi.org/10.1038/d41586-019-01797-0 This article is about English as a universal language for science and the problems it has for people who are not native English speakers and therefore may miss opportunities that create barriers in their professional careers. Also, it explains the reasons why English has become a language that has a good vocabulary for science that sometimes is not in other languages, and we can better describe what we want to expose because it is "precise and direct".

Hu, B. (2010). The challenges of Chinese: a preliminary study of UK learners’ perceptions of difficulty. Language Learning Journal, 38(1), 99–118. https://doi.org/10.1080/09571731003620721 In this study is shown the results of a survey where the most difficult aspects of Chinese for Chinese learners in the UK were evaluated using a survey divided in 3 parts: Information about the student's learning (self-assessment, learning styles, age, etc). The second part assessed their language skills (speaking, reading, listening and writing) and in the third part they rated how difficult they found the 34 most difficult aspects of Chinese on a Likert scale from 1 to 5. The aspects were divided into Grammar, Aural Reception, Words, Pronunciation, Oral production, and Recall.

Castro, O., & Peck, V. (2005). Learning Styles and Foreign Language Learning Difficulties. Foreign Language Annals, 38(3), 401–409. https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1944-9720.2005.tb02226.x

Yang, X., & Duan, Y. (2016). Difficulties of Chinese students with their academic English: Evidence from a China-United States university program. In this article we analyze the difficulties in learning English through interviews with Chinese students learning English being evaluated in writing, speaking, reading and writing.

OSUNA, M. D. (2011). "FACTORS INVOLVED IN SECOND LANGUAGE LEARNING". https://archivos.csif.es/archivos/andalucia/ensenanza/revistas/iee/Numero_46/MARIA_DEL_CARMEN_RUBIO_OSUNA_02.pdf This article talks about the different factors that we can find when learning a language, divided into general and individual ones, and tries to express the most basic ways that we as learners have to learn a foreign language.
